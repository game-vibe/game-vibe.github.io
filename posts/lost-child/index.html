Game Description#

Lost Child begins with Andrey’s awakening inside the endless labyrinth of corridors. His memory is erased, and the physical space around is distorted with each movement. Voices are reported from darkness, accompanied by childish screams and the appearance of ominous shadows. These phenomena give rise to uncertainty in their source: whether it is hallucinations, scraps of the past or someone’s cruel experiment. The further path is a study of this anxious and dynamic environment.

The central conflict in Lost Child rotates around the fragmented past prostagonist and oppressive, reflecting his situation. He moves through the changing halls to collect the dark secret from the scattered remnants of his story together. The corridors live their lives, wriggling and rebuilt in direct dependence on the internal state and fears of the hero. In addition, thin details and references to real events are built into the world, designed to make the player think, and the main goal is to reveal this hidden truth.

Progress requires a clash with the contents behind closed doors, each of which stores part of the information that are unwanted to Andrei. The plot establishes that the escape from personal history is impossible here. Sound signals, including a whisper from invisible sources and an alarming echo, make a significant contribution to the pressure atmosphere. The main game experience is built on this study and the gradual, inevitable process of restoration in the memory of events, which cannot be forgotten.
