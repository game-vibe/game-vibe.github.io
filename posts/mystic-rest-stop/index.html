Game Description#

Mystic Rest Stop offers a unique twist on the psychological horror genre. The title follows 22-year-old Arthur Brooks, who takes a summer job working the night shift at a lonely gas station. When he gets used to the role, it seems to him that something is wrong. There is an uneasy tension in the atmosphere that continues as customers come and go. But these aren’t just ordinary clients: Arthur’s encounters quickly escalate, blurring the lines between the mundane and the horrific. It’s a night shift that gets darker by the hour.

Mystic Rest Stop’s gameplay revolves around first-person interaction, with Arthur performing mundane tasks such as serving customers and preparing food. However, during these actions the feeling of anxiety does not leave. The environments are fully interactive, allowing players to explore and uncover creepy details as the story progresses. The structure also includes moments of relaxation in the house, where users can receive messages from NPCs or simply reflect on the strange events unfolding at the station.

Whether driving through deep forests or managing a quiet, secluded station, gamers will encounter strange stories based on real-life urban legends. These short, powerful stories immerse you in fears that feel all too real, making each shift more unsettling than the last. The tension is growing every moment, and users are wondering what is really going on around Arthur.
