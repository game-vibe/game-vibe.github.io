Game Description#

Ambrosia Sky plunges into the atmosphere of a deserted colony near the rings of Saturn, where an alien fungal infection has turned human habitation into a disaster zone. After 15 years of absence, Dalia returns here, a disaster response specialist and a member of the Scarab caste - space caretakers who see off the dead on their final journey. Her task is not only to clean up the area, but also to conduct a bioremediation ritual over the bodies of the dead, such as her old friend Gerald Parker. This procedure allows the remains to be transformed into a new form of life, paying tribute to the victims of the tragedy.

The whole process takes place from the first person, and the main tool of the heroine is a multifunctional sprayer. It can be used to wash away growths, burn out dangerous spores, or apply a special foam that can conduct electricity to activate consoles and restore power supply. Ambrosia Sky combines exploring damaged corridors and reading notes with the need to solve environmental puzzles. For example, turning off gravity opens up new routes for moving around the station, and a grappling hook helps you overcome chasms and destroyed passages.

Arsenal of a bioremediation specialist:

Cleaning surfaces of fungal colonies with a powerful jet of water. Burning out particularly dangerous spores using the fire mode. Creation of conductive foam to restore power to de-energized systems. Study the behavior of different strains to create equipment improvements.

The alien fungus is not a passive contamination, but a living and aggressive threat, capable of consuming entire rooms in seconds and attacking Dahlia. Some of its species release electrical discharges, while others try to trap them. Despite the melancholy theme, the clearing experience feels intense and kinetic, requiring gamers to adapt and inventively survive.
