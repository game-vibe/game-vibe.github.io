Game Description#

Blasfemia traps the player in a seemingly ordinary apartment, experienced entirely from a first-person perspective. At first, the room looks ordinary, but it soon becomes clear that the gamer is not alone here. An unknown presence makes itself felt, creating a feeling of constant surveillance, which makes the walls seem to shrink. The main task is to find a way out of the residence while a certain entity prevents the escape, and its proximity is felt in every room.

As the house is explored, Blasfemia’s backstory is revealed through the surroundings and the finds made. Objects and clues found shed light on the details of the tragedy that occurred here, explaining the current situation and telling about the former residents. This knowledge is extremely important, because the strength and visibility of the antagonist increases as the game progresses. There are no weapons in this game, so a person is completely defenseless against the intangible force that pursues him in the corridors.

The only way to survive is to outwit the enemy, since direct confrontation is impossible. Progress through the story is achieved by interacting with objects, collecting things and solving puzzles built into the structure of the apartments. These puzzles test observation and logic while the character is under direct threat. Each action has weight, leading to one of two endings: a successful escape from the apartment or the final completion of the story within these walls.
