Game Description#

Fish to Dish: Idle Sushi is an idle fishing game designed to run on the player’s desktop, taking up part of the screen. The boat floats on digital waves, a line is cast, and a variety of marine life is caught from various bays and islands. The gameplay combines collecting creatures with managing a food establishment. The mechanism deploys as the vessel crosses the water, automatically pulling out the prey at a preset speed. This game runs continuously in the background of other computer activities.

All catches become potential ingredients for the sushi bar in Fish to Dish: Idle Sushi. Gamers take raw fish and other sea animals to prepare dishes, which are then sold to a stream of customers for gold. This currency is a mechanism for purchasing fishing rod upgrades and unlocking new types of bait to hunt specific species. The money earned also finances the expansion of the establishment itself, allowing for the addition of furniture and decorations. The main goal is to fill the bestiary with records of every creature encountered.

The digital ocean contains a diverse assortment of creatures, from ordinary fish to much larger animals found in deep waters. Other objects, including starfish, turtles and even rubber ducks, can also be collected from the surface. A completed bestiary documents each specimen obtained throughout the sessions. In addition to the commercial component, you can build a personal aquarium, filling it with your finds to create a closed ecosystem. Customers include a variety of visitors, and the business may also attract felines.
