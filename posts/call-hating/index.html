Game Description#

In Call Hating, an aging and forgetful employee working from the house is faced with a technological crisis after a smartphone breakdown and the Internet disconnect. The main task of the player is to use outdated equipment from the home office to pass the hellish telephone menu. The path to restoring the connection lies through interaction with the acquired technology and solve a series of problems. This mission is served as an urgent need, and the goal must be achieved at all costs. The test design and the general atmosphere of the project are inspired by the retro-stylistic of the DOS era.

The Call Hating game process is a homework simulator in a quest room in format, supplemented by mini-games and black humor. The narrative uses comments breaking the fourth wall, drawing inspiration from plots of Titles such as The Stanley Parable and Portal. The plot is promoted through the Point-And-Click interaction and the solution of the puzzles built into the environment. The combination of these elements creates a unique test for a gamer striving for the main goal. This Adventure is an interactive parody of modern difficulties.

The study of the home environment is necessary to search for hidden objects and overcome plot obstacles. The decisions made directly affect the outcome, directing the narrative to one of several available endings. Such a non -linear structure is a key element of the adventure, making each choice to the same. The whole game is permeated with a sense of constant emotional confusion. The final of history depends on the specific actions committed in overcoming technological and bureaucratic barriers.
