Game Description#

The Roottrees are Dead invites gamers to immerse themselves in the intricacies of a family history shrouded in the mystery of tragedy. Set against the backdrop of the December 1998 plane crash that claimed the lives of the Roottry family, the title challenges users to uncover the mysteries surrounding a famous candy-making family. Through research and deduction, participants embark on a quest to piece together a complex family tree, identifying both known and little-known relatives. The plot centers on Elias and Gwyneth Roottry, whose descendants' lives are intertwined with a tragic event. Mystery invites gamers to explore a world where history, secrets and relationships are far from simple.

At its core, The Roottrees are Dead is a puzzle game, but not in the traditional sense. Users don’t just navigate through preset levels or fight enemies. Instead, they become digital detectives, diving into archives and sifting through layers of information. Their task? Reconstruct the Rottrey family tree by collecting the names, professions and personal details of each family member. The process is methodical, almost like a hunt for historical clues, with each detail adding context to the larger narrative. To find the missing puzzle pieces, gamers must use a variety of research tools, including a search engine, the local library’s online catalog, and a periodical database.
